description = 'Examines urban growth boundaries adopted by over 100 U.S. cities and counties, finding they reduce the supply of developable land and increase housing prices. Portland experienced dramatic affordability declines between 1990 and 1995, with lot prices more than doubling while consumer prices rose 52.5%, transforming Portland from one of the most affordable to one of the least affordable housing markets on the West Coast. Recommends market-oriented alternatives including relaxing zoning density restrictions and pricing infrastructure at full marginal cost.'
