Authorizes cities and counties to adopt property tax exemptions for homeowners who rent space in their residences through home share programs to low-income individuals, with a 500-home statewide cap and sunset in 2029.

[Original Bill Text](https://olis.oregonlegislature.gov/liz/2019R1/Measures/Overview/SB1045)

## Overview

Authorizes city or county to adopt property tax exemption 	<i>for portion</i>] <b>in amount not to exceed $300,000 of assessed value</b> of principal place of residence used by homeowner to rent space in residence <b>through public or nonprofit home share program</b> to individuals seeking such space. 	<i>Provides that exemption shall be granted as 100 percent exemption for five consecutive years unless city or county adopts other terms.</i>] Provides that 	<i>city or county</i>] <b>adopting jurisdiction</b> must require 	<i>means testing of home share seekers. Requires</i>] home share rental agreement to offer space at rent affordable to home share seekers with income at or below 	<i>level determined by city or county</i>] <b>60 percent of area median income, must prohibit exemption for home share agreements among family members and must specify period for which exemption may be granted</b>. 
Provides that 	<i>exemption applies only to tax levy of city or county enacting exemption law and other taxing districts agreeing to grant exemption,</i>] <b>ordinance or resolution granting exemption may not take effect</b> unless rates of taxation of <b>adopting jurisdiction and</b> taxing districts that agree to grant exemption equal 51 percent or more of total combined rate of taxation. 	<i>Requires exemption to be granted to all residences on same terms in effect on date application is submitted.</i>] 
Prohibits property receiving any other property tax benefit, other than homestead property tax deferral, from being granted home share exemption. Allows 	<i>city or county</i>] <b>adopting jurisdiction</b> to amend 	<i>or terminate</i>] exemption, subject to approval process of taxing districts described above, <b>or repeal exemption,</b> but provides that residences granted exemption continue to receive exemption under terms in effect at time exemption was first granted. 
<b>Sets cap of 500 homes that may be granted exemption statewide in any property tax year.</b> 
Sunsets authority of city or county to adopt exemption 	<i>or deferral on June 30</i>] <b>that becomes effective after January 31</b>, 2027. Repeals exemption program on January 2, 2029. Provides that residences granted exemption before repeal date continue to receive exemption 	<i>under terms in effect at time granted</i>] <b>if term extends past repeal date</b>. 
Takes effect on 91st day following adjournment sine die.
