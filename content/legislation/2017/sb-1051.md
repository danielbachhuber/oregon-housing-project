# SB 1051 - Fast-Track and ADUs

In response to the housing supply shortage, SB 1051 (2017) streamlined approvals and expanded housing options. It requires cities and counties to approve affordable housing developments of â‰¥5 units within 100 days (faster than the usual 120-day land use clock) when at least half the units are affordable at 60% MFI or below. It also updated the definition of "needed housing" to include affordable units, reinforcing clear-and-objective approval standards.

Critically, SB 1051 legalized Accessory Dwelling Units (ADUs) in all single-family zones statewide (for cities >2,500) and allowed religious institutions to build affordable housing on their land by right. These reforms removed local barriers to smaller infill housing and faith-based affordable projects, leading to more ADUs and creative development in urban areas.
