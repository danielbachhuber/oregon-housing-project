Extends the grace period for repayment of residential rent accrued during the COVID-19 emergency and prohibits landlords from reporting or screening based on that debt.

[Original Bill Text](https://olis.oregonlegislature.gov/liz/2021R1/Measures/Overview/SB282)

## Overview

<i>Directs Housing and Community Services Department to study issues related to rental housing and report to appropriate committee or interim committee of Legislative Assembly on or before September 15, 2022.</i>] 
<i>Sunsets December 31, 2022.</i>] 
<b>Extends grace period for repayment of residential rent accrued during emergency period of April 1, 2020, to June 30, 2021, until February 28, 2022. 
Prohibits residential landlord from reporting nonpayment of rent accrued during emergency period to credit bureaus. Prohibits residential landlord from screening prospective tenant based on nonpayment of rent accrued during grace period. Allows certain defendants to set aside and seal judgments of evictions based on claims arising during grace period. 
Limits certain restrictions on residential tenant's guests. Sunsets March 1, 2022. 
Declares emergency, effective on passage.</b>
