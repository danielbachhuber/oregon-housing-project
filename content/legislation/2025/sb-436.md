This bill provides that constructing a nonprofit child care facility within an affordable housing project does not subject the housing project to prevailing wage requirements if the facility meets specified criteria.

## Overview

Digest: Says that building a child care center in a place that provides affordable housing does not mean that a person building the whole place must pay prevailing wage. But building the child care center itself does require paying prevailing wage for labor. (Flesch Readability Score: 62.6). 
Provides that constructing a nonprofit child care facility within a project for affordable housing does not subject the project for affordable housing to prevailing wage requirements if the child care facility meets specified requirements. Provides that prevailing wage requirements do apply to the construction of a child care facility. 
Takes effect on the 91st day following adjournment sine die.
