Ivory Mathews, CEO of [Home Forward](/organizations/home-forward)—the state's largest public housing authority serving Multnomah County—is resigning following reporting that revealed she spent over $100,000 on agency-funded travel while the organization's vacancy rate spiked to 15% and buildings failed to meet debt service ratios. The resignation comes after mounting pressure from the union representing 205 employees who declared "no confidence" in Mathews, half of [Portland](/cities/portland) City Council expressing concerns, and scrutiny over work trips including a 6-day Hawaii trip with no proof of work-related activity. Despite the Home Forward board defending Mathews' $342,000-a-year leadership, the agency left 955 units vacant with six-month average fill times even as 7,500 people slept on [Portland](/cities/portland)'s streets nightly.
