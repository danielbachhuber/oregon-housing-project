U.S. Sen. Ron Wyden and U.S. Rep. Val Hoyle introduced the DASH Act to address Oregon's housing crisis through tax credits for first-time homebuyers, landlords renting to low-income tenants, and construction subsidies for developers building affordable housing. The legislation responds to Oregon's 19% increase in homelessness and the need to build nearly 30,000 housing units annually to address the state's shortage. This year's version improves upon previous proposals by providing the homebuyer tax credit at closing rather than at tax filing time and offering deductions for homeowners who sell at a loss.
