Housing starts fell to 1.17 million in May, the lowest level since April 2020, representing an 8.5% drop from May 2025, according to the U.S. Census Bureau. The decline was most severe in the South (down 15%) and West (down 11%), though the Northeast saw a 19% increase, raising concerns about worsening housing shortages nationwide. Builder confidence has declined due to higher material and financing costs, threatening recent state-level progress in constructing adequate housing for new residents.
