Lloyd Center mall in Northeast [Portland](/cities/portland) will close to the public on August 8, with remaining tenants vacating by August 31, as the 20-block property moves forward with redevelopment plans for mixed-use housing, retail, and open space. Developer Urban Renaissance Group says the mall is over 90% vacant and "no longer viable in its current form," though the Save Lloyd campaign has filed two appeals against the city's approval, with a Portland City Council hearing scheduled for June 24. Critics argue the closure announcement while appeals are pending forces out remaining businesses and perpetuates a "dead mall" narrative.
