[Home Forward](/organizations/home-forward) CEO Ivory Mathews resigned April 29 following reports of lavish travel spending, receiving a $171,424 severance package that sparked calls from [Portland](/cities/portland) officials including Mayor [Keith Wilson](/people/keith-wilson) and City Councilors for the agency's board to resign and undergo top-to-bottom reform. The housing authority, which provides 7,000 affordable apartments and over 10,000 federal rent assistance vouchers to Multnomah County residents, faces ongoing problems including more than 900 vacant units, financial distress in its property portfolio, and tenant safety concerns that predate Mathews' tenure. While the board has pledged to bring in consultants and create a public dashboard for vacancy data, critics including union representatives and elected officials argue that leadership changes alone won't address systemic issues like slow unit turnover, inadequate security, and the agency's continued expansion despite struggling to maintain existing properties.
