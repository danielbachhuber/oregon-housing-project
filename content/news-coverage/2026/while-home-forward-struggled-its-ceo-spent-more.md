[Portland](/cities/portland)'s [Home Forward](/organizations/home-forward) CEO Ivory Mathews spent over $100,000 on taxpayer-funded travel between 2023-2025, attending primarily National Association of Housing and Redevelopment Officials conferences while campaigning for a leadership position, even as the agency struggled with 14% vacancy rates, deteriorating living conditions, and worsening finances. City Councilor Eric Zimmerman criticized Mathews' extensive travel during the agency's crisis, while Home Forward's board chair defended her national engagement as essential to connecting with federal policy decisions that impact the agency's funding. The controversy comes as Home Forward, which owns 7,000 low-income apartments and serves roughly 12,000 Multnomah County residents, faces declining federal support, with half of tenants more than 30 days delinquent on rent and its debt service coverage ratio dropping from 2.36 in 2020 to 1.12 in 2025.
