[Portland](/cities/portland)'s housing authority [Home Forward](/organizations/home-forward) faces crisis as its CEO Ivory Mathews resigned following reports she spent over $100,000 on agency-funded travel, including an undocumented Hawaii trip, while the agency struggled and employees expressed no confidence in her leadership. The controversy erupted after 205 unionized employees and city councilors criticized Mathews' spending, though the board of commissioners had initially defended the travel as necessary for her role. The resignation comes amid broader scrutiny of the housing authority's fiscal management during a period when it faced organizational challenges.
