Housing shortages have eased in most states since 2020 due to new construction making apartments and houses more affordable, though Connecticut, New Jersey, and Rhode Island remain exceptions where housing supply lags behind population growth. The increased apartment supply helped drive down the nation's median rent by 1.7% in April compared to last year, with the most dramatic improvements in affordability occurring in the South and Midwest. New Jersey faces particular challenges with approximately 260,000 new residents but only 66,500 new housing units added since 2020, as the state's efforts to impose affordable housing quotas on towns have met strong suburban resistance.
