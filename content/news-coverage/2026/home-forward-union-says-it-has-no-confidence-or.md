The union representing 205 [Home Forward](/organizations/home-forward) staff has declared "no confidence or trust" in agency leadership after revelations that CEO Ivory Mathews spent over $100,000 on travel between 2023-2025, including a $7,269 Hawaii trip that doubled as a family vacation, while employees were required to take 40-hour furloughs to address budget deficits. AFSCME Local 3135 criticized the [Portland](/cities/portland) housing authority's Board of Commissioners for defending Mathews' spending and 59% salary increase even as the agency struggles with vacant affordable housing units and a $13.8 million budget shortfall. Mayor [Keith Wilson](/people/keith-wilson) said he is "disturbed" by the agency's performance and expects "clear, measurable improvement" in the responsible use of taxpayer funds.
