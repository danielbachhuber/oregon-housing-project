State lawmakers across the country are increasingly working across party lines to pass pro-housing legislation, with 124 pro-housing bills clearing state legislatures from July 2024 through June 2025, up from 40 in 2022-2023. The bipartisan measures include allowing manufactured homes in single-family zones (passed in Florida, Idaho, and Virginia), easing regulations on accessory dwelling units (enacted in Arizona, Arkansas, Iowa, Maryland, New Hampshire, and Virginia), and permitting housing on church-owned land through "Yes In God's Backyard" legislation in Virginia. Housing advocates credit the rare bipartisan cooperation to messaging that combines left-leaning priorities of affordability and equity with right-leaning values of property rights and markets, with urban progressives, rural conservatives, and libertarians finding common ground on the crisis.
