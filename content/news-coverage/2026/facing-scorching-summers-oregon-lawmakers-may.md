Oregon lawmakers are considering expanding state heat pump programs nearly five years after the 2021 Pacific Northwest heat dome killed over 100 Oregonians, with State Rep. Pam Marsh planning to convene a workgroup on cooling systems in housing as [Gov. Tina Kotek](/people/tina-kotek) declared a wildfire emergency amid worsening heat and drought. While lawmakers allocated nearly $25 million in 2022 and $4 million in 2024 for heat pump rebates, the programs have been paused due to lack of funding despite estimates that fixing the cooling gap for the 58% of Oregonians without permanent cooling systems would cost $500 million to $1 billion. Oregon has installed only 4,638 heat pumps through state incentive programs toward its goal of 500,000 by 2030, with advocates urging renewed legislative funding to help low-income families access energy-efficient cooling as climate change drives hotter summers statewide.
