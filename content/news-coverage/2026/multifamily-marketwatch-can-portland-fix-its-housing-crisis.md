[Portland](/cities/portland) housing advocates and providers are addressing the city's housing crisis through a new public exhibition called "Dear Portland" that humanizes homelessness through video portraits and data about the region's persistent underbuilding. Michael Larson of Humans for Housing and Megan Howard of Tandem Properties discuss how government policies, shrinking budgets, and inadequate wraparound services have created a situation where four people enter homelessness for every three who are housed, with over 17,000 currently homeless in Multnomah County. The exhibit notably avoids demonizing housing providers, instead highlighting how rising costs (property taxes, insurance, utilities) drive rent increases while [Portland](/cities/portland) loses major employers, creating an economic crisis where wages can't keep pace with housing costs. Both advocates emphasize that housing alone isn't sufficient—people with chronic homelessness need case management and behavioral health services that are increasingly threatened by budget cuts and shelter closures.
