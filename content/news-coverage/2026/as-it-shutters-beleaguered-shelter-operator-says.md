Nonprofit shelter operator Sunstone Way announced it will lay off all 175 employees by June 30 as it shuts down operations in [Portland](/cities/portland), closing multiple city-funded homeless shelters including a 96-bed Centennial facility and a 38-unit pod complex in the Lloyd District. The closure follows a lawsuit by the organization's former finance director alleging fiscal mismanagement, including overspending on office space, staff retreats, and overpriced contracts with connected nonprofits—claims Sunstone Way has denied. The mass layoffs highlight the broader challenges facing homeless services workers as government funding cuts and rising costs destabilize the sector.
