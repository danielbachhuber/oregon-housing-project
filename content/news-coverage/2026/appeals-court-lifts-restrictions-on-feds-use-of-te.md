A federal appeals court has lifted restrictions on federal agents' use of tear gas and crowd control weapons near an ICE facility on [Portland](/cities/portland)'s South Waterfront, reversing lower court rulings that had severely limited such tactics following incidents where chemical munitions affected protesters, journalists, and residents of the nearby Gray's Landing affordable housing complex. The 9th U.S. Circuit Court of Appeals' three-judge panel consolidated two cases—Dickinson v. Trump and Reach Community Development v. US Department of Homeland Security—and granted an administrative stay to "maintain the status quo" while the court deliberates further, with oral arguments scheduled for April 7. The decision comes amid ongoing controversy over federal agents' use of force against protesters, including a February incident where agents tear-gassed a crowd that included children.
