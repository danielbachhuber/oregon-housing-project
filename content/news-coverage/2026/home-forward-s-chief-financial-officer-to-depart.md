[Home Forward](/organizations/home-forward)'s Chief Financial Officer Kandy Sage will depart on July 2, following the April resignation of CEO Ivory Mathews amid controversy over expensive travel and organizational mismanagement. The housing authority, which owns 7,000 low-income apartments and provides rent assistance to 12,000 Multnomah County residents, has struggled with vacant units, financial difficulties, and an inability to fill apartments despite chronic homelessness in [Portland](/cities/portland). Sage oversaw finances during challenging times including the pandemic, inflation, and the agency's 900+ vacant units that worsened its financial situation.
