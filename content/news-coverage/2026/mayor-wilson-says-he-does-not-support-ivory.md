[Portland](/cities/portland) Mayor [Keith Wilson](/people/keith-wilson) publicly opposed the $171,420 severance package for departing [Home Forward](/organizations/home-forward) CEO Ivory Mathews, who resigned after reports of extensive agency-funded travel, though he lacks authority to block the board's decision. The Home Forward board faces calls to resign from Multnomah County Commissioner Shannon Singleton and City Councilor Eric Zimmerman for its handling of the controversy, including defending Mathews despite a union vote of no confidence. Wilson appoints board members and said he is meeting with potential appointees to improve accountability at the affordable housing agency, which has two vacant board seats.
