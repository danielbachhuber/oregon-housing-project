Oregon reached a $7 million settlement with LivCor, a major property management company that used RealPage software to artificially inflate rent prices on nearly 1,650 Oregon properties by sharing sensitive rental data with competitors instead of competing on price. Attorney General Dan Rayfield, along with eight other state attorneys general, ordered LivCor to stop using the rent-fixing algorithm and cooperate with ongoing litigation against RealPage and other property management companies including Camden, Pinnacle, and Willow Bridge. This marks the second such settlement after a similar $7 million agreement with Greystar in November 2024, part of a broader multistate effort to combat rental price collusion disguised as algorithmic pricing.
