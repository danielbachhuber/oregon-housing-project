Federal data shows U.S. homelessness declined 3% from 2024 to 2025, but Oregon saw a 19% increase, the second-highest jump among states after North Carolina's hurricane-related surge. The Trump administration cited the overall 27% increase in homelessness since 2013 as evidence that housing-first policies have failed, while announcing a shift toward transitional housing with work and treatment requirements. The count found about 745,652 homeless people nationwide, with decreases driven by fewer people in emergency shelters and unsheltered populations.
