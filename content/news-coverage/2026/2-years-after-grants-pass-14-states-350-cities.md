Two years after the Supreme Court's Grants Pass v. Johnson decision allowed enforcement of public camping bans, more than 350 cities and 14 states have adopted stricter laws on street homelessness, including statewide camping bans and requirements for local enforcement. States like Louisiana, Indiana, Georgia, and Oklahoma have enacted varying approaches, from criminalizing unauthorized camping to allowing property owners to sue local governments for failing to enforce bans, with some legislation modeled after conservative think tank proposals. Despite these crackdowns, homelessness increased in 28 states between January 2024 and January 2025, according to federal data.
