Nearly 1,000 Oregon households used the state's Flex Lending program to purchase homes in 2025, with 33 state-approved lenders providing almost $300 million through [Oregon Housing and Community Services](/organizations/oregon-housing-and-community-services). The program, which began development in 2018, helps low- to moderate-income borrowers buy homes with minimal upfront cash and lower interest rates, serving first-time homebuyers through FirstHome and non-homeowners earning $125,000 or less through NextStep. About 31% of participants identified as people of color, 32% were veterans, and 43% lived in rural areas, as Oregon lawmakers work toward their 2030 goal of reaching 65% homeownership statewide.
