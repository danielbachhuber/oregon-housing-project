[Portland](/cities/portland) Mayor [Keith Wilson](/people/keith-wilson) warned the City Council that affordable housing providers could face insolvency within six months due to issues including tenant nonpayment, criminal activity, and high vacancy rates, with the city's housing authority [Home Forward](/organizations/home-forward) serving as a prime example after its CEO resigned in April amid financial troubles. Wilson urged the council to tighten screening and eviction policies around criminal activity, revise the Fair Access in Renting Ordinance, and establish stable funding for affordable housing, while committing to improve building security and fill the Portland Housing Bureau director position. The mayor's letter highlights the crisis facing [Portland](/cities/portland)'s affordable housing system, where drug trafficking and building takeovers by squatters threaten to destabilize properties housing the city's most vulnerable residents.
