[Portland](/cities/portland)'s housing authority [Home Forward](/organizations/home-forward) cannot provide documentation of CEO Ivory Mathews' $7,269 six-day Hawaii trip in October 2024 for a captive insurance conference, despite spending over $100,000 on CEO travel in three years. AFSCME Local 3135, representing 205 agency staff, declared "no confidence" in Mathews' leadership during an April 21 board meeting where union members questioned the travel spending while staff struggle financially, and Mayor [Keith Wilson](/people/keith-wilson) expressed being "disturbed" by the agency's performance. The controversy has prompted questions about whether Home Forward will relocate its insurance captive from Hawaii to states with better-regarded domiciles like Arizona, Vermont, or Utah.
