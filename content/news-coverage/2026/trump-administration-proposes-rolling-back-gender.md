The Trump administration's U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development has proposed a rule that would eliminate gender identity protections in federally funded housing and shelters by replacing "gender identity" with "sex" defined as biological classification, reversing an Obama-era policy. The National Housing Law Project warns the measure would force shelters to deny access to transgender people and allow discrimination, potentially increasing homelessness among a population already experiencing disproportionate housing insecurity. The proposal could trigger legal conflicts between the federal government and states like California, Illinois, Massachusetts, and New York that have enacted their own gender identity housing protections.
