[Portland](/cities/portland) Mayor [Keith Wilson](/people/keith-wilson)'s home-sharing pilot program, launched in February with the Portland Housing Bureau, has received only five applications in four months despite offering homeowners $1,000 to rent spare bedrooms for $200 or less per month. Wilson touted the program as "one of the fastest and most cost-effective ways" to provide housing and end unsheltered homelessness when he announced it after traveling to Atlanta to meet with PadSplit founders. The low participation rate raises questions about the program's viability, with only a third of the one-year pilot remaining.
