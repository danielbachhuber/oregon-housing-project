The U.S. Forest Service evicted hundreds of homeless people from the "China Hat" encampment in Deschutes National Forest near Bend in May 2024, leaving over 100 vehicles and countless personal belongings behind, with former residents saying the agency failed to provide adequate opportunity to reclaim their property despite a 90-day retrieval window. Property law experts told InvestigateWest and FORJournalism that the Forest Service appears to have fallen short of constitutional obligations, with residents reporting unanswered phone calls, missed appointments, and blanket denials for "small items" recovery. The closure, possibly the largest encampment sweep on forestland to date, displaced 200-400 people in an area where homelessness has more than doubled since 2017 as housing prices in Bend skyrocketed by nearly 75% over two decades.
