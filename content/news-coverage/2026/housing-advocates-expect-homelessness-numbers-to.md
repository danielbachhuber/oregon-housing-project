A new report from nonprofit advocacy group Community Solutions projects that U.S. homelessness may decline by roughly 2% in 2025 to about 755,300 people, following a record-high surge of 18% in 2024 that brought the total to 771,480. The projection is based on preliminary Point-in-Time count data from 170 communities collected in January, though HUD has not yet released official 2025 figures. The report suggests homelessness has stabilized after years of rapid expansion, with entries into homelessness now more closely balanced with exits.
