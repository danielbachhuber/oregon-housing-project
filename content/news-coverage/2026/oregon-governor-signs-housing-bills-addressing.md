[Gov. Tina Kotek](/people/tina-kotek) signed six housing bills into law, including measures that allow one-time [urban growth boundary](/key-concepts#urban-growth-boundary) expansions for cities to add manufactured housing and senior housing developments, with cities over 25,000 receiving up to 100 additional acres and smaller cities up to 50 acres. The legislation also establishes a $20 million loan fund through [Oregon Housing and Community Services](/organizations/oregon-housing-and-community-services) to incentivize mixed-income housing development and creates new programs to preserve existing affordable housing at risk of losing federal affordability restrictions. The bills aim to help Oregon meet its goal of building 29,500-36,000 homes annually to address the state's housing shortage, concentrated primarily in the [Portland](/cities/portland) region and Willamette Valley.
