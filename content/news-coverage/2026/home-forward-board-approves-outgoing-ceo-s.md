The [Home Forward](/organizations/home-forward) board unanimously approved a $171,000 severance package for former CEO Ivory Mathews, who resigned amid controversy over travel spending, while also approving a $275,000 salary for incoming interim director Michael Buonocore from the [Portland](/cities/portland) Housing Bureau. Staff and union representatives sharply criticized the generous severance—which includes six months' pay, health insurance, vacation payout, and up to $50,000 for job search assistance—as insulting given that all employees face 40 hours of unpaid furlough this year and a recently laid-off worker received only one week's pay. The decision has intensified calls for accountability at the affordable housing agency, with local elected officials and union leadership demanding the board's resignation for failing to provide adequate oversight while Mathews allegedly misused public funds.
