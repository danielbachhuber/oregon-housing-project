Multnomah County has suspended reimbursement payments to troubled shelter contractor Sunstone Way for April, May, and June while conducting a fiscal compliance review, withholding funds to ensure recovery of any misspent dollars before the nonprofit shuts down June 30. The suspension threatens severance and paid time off for 175 laid-off workers who staffed three pod villages and two homeless shelters, with Oregon AFSCME warning members that a near-final severance agreement depended on the now-frozen county payments. Sunstone Way, formerly All Good Northwest, announced its closure in March after its former finance manager sued for retaliation over questioned spending decisions.
