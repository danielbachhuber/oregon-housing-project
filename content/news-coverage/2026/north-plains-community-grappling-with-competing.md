The small Washington County town of North Plains (population 3,500) is debating whether to expand its [Urban Growth Boundary (UGB)](/key-concepts#urban-growth-boundary), with competing visions dividing civic leaders and residents over how the community should grow and develop its tax base. A community meeting at Atfalati Ridge Elementary School drew 55 residents who shared differing perspectives on preserving the town's character versus planning for population and economic growth. Key participants included library director Robin Doughty (later named interim city manager), assistant planner Rowan Maiorano, and representatives from various community groups including Friends of Smart Growth North Plains.
