Oregon's [Urban Growth Boundary (UGB)](/key-concepts#urban-growth-boundary) system effectively manages land for housing and development, with 96% of UGB expansion requests approved from 2016-March 2025, according to advocates defending the land use program against critics. The state's primary obstacles to housing and industrial development are infrastructure funding gaps—such as Bend's $101 million transportation shortfall—and lack of subsidies for affordable housing, not land availability within existing UGBs. Recent updates include the Oregon Housing Needs Analysis requiring cities over 10,000 population to inventory buildable land and plan for housing across five income categories with 20-year projections.
