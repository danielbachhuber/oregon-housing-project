Multnomah County found that Sunstone Way, a homeless shelter provider, misspent $3.6 million in public funds between July 2024 and February 2026 on unallowable expenses including staff entertainment, alcohol, and unapproved payroll costs, according to a June 16 letter from county officials. The nonprofit, which operated three pod villages and two homeless shelters in the [Portland](/cities/portland) area, announced it would cease operations and lay off all 175 staff members by June 30, citing inadequate government funding—though this followed mounting scrutiny over financial practices including a 2022 audit that found $525,000 in overbilling. County Chair Vega Pederson demanded accountability for what she called "extremely irresponsible" use of public dollars, while Homeless Services Department Director Nathaniel VerGow committed to improved oversight going forward.
