[Portland](/cities/portland)'s underutilized Willamette River waterfront is the focus of revitalization efforts led by Design Portland's Randy Gragg, who proposes transforming downtown into a residential neighborhood by converting vacant office buildings and creating river amenities like playgrounds and floating restaurants. The push follows successful waterfront developments in other cities and Portland suburb Vancouver, Washington, which opened a $1.3 billion waterfront park in 2018, while Portland's riverfront remains hampered by infrastructure like the 1920s seawall and Interstate 5. Efforts are complicated by 10 miles of Superfund pollution from the Broadway Bridge to Sauvie Island, though advocates see cleanup as an opportunity to restore habitats and improve public access to the water.
