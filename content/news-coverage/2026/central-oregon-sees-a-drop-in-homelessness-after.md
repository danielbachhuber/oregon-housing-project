Central Oregon saw a 19.1% drop in homelessness between 2025 and 2026, declining from about 2,100 to nearly 1,700 individuals, according to a point-in-time count celebrated by [Gov. Tina Kotek](/people/tina-kotek) and local housing advocates. The decline contrasts with an 18.9% statewide increase in homelessness between 2024 and 2025, and advocates attribute the regional success to Kotek's executive orders declaring a homelessness state of emergency and increased housing investments. Housing affordability remains a key challenge in the region, with Deschutes County's median home price at $675,000, nearly $150,000 above Oregon's median.
