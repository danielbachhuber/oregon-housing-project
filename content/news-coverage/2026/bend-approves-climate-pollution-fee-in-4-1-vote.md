Bend City Council approved a climate pollution fee in a 4-1 vote that will charge up to $2,972 for gas appliance installations in new homes starting April 2027, making it Oregon's second city after Ashland to implement such a policy aimed at reducing greenhouse gas emissions. The fee, supported by advocacy group Energize Bend after four years of effort, faced opposition from power companies like Cascade Natural Gas Corporation, builders, and union members who argued it would hurt housing affordability in a city that needs to build over 15,700 homes in eight years to address its housing shortfall. Mayor Pro Tem Megan Perkins cast the lone dissenting vote, while supporters said the policy helps Bend meet its 2016 climate action resolution and aligns with state residential energy codes taking effect next year.
