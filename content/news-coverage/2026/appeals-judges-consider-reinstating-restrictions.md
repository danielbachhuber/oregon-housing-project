Federal appeals judges are reconsidering their earlier decision to allow federal officers to use tear gas and other force on protesters outside the [Portland](/cities/portland) ICE facility, with the three-judge panel appearing disturbed by video evidence of officers' conduct during hearings in San Francisco. The cases involve protesters, journalists, and residents of a low-income apartment building near the facility who claim they have been targeted with excessive force and chemical munitions during largely peaceful demonstrations that have occurred since June 2025. The judges, including Trump appointees Kenneth Lee and Eric Tung and Biden appointee Ana de Alba, are expected to issue a decision before mid-April that will govern federal officers' use of force at the site.
