[Metro](/organizations/metro) President Lynn Peterson's early departure has left a contested race, with Metro Councilor Juan Carlos Gonzalez emerging as the frontrunner to lead an agency facing mounting challenges including expiring homeless services funding, budget shortfalls, and declining public confidence. The agency oversees regional planning plus a $2.5 billion homeless services tax set to expire in 2030, with serious doubts about renewal given poor accountability for spending results. Willamette Week endorses Gonzalez for Metro President, Ashton Simpson for his eastside district seat, and Alex Phan for the District 4 vacancy left by Gonzalez's presidential bid.
