[Portland](/cities/portland) City Council approved $2.5 million in funding for two Black-led nonprofits to build affordable housing in historically Black neighborhoods, with Self Enhancement Inc. receiving $1.5 million for 49 homes in East Vanport and Williams & Russell CDC getting $1 million for 20 townhomes and 85 apartments in Albina. The ordinance, championed by [Loretta Smith](/people/loretta-smith), aims to address racial homeownership disparities—only 30% of Black Oregon households own homes compared to 67% of white households—and repair damage from mid-20th century displacement caused by urban renewal projects. The funding comes from the Construction Excise Tax Sub-fund after months of debate, passing 10-2 despite concerns from two councilors about the selection process.
