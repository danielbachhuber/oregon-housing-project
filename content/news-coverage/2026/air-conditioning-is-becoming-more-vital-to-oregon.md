Rising temperatures from climate change have made air conditioning a public health necessity in Oregon, with 82% of housing units now equipped with cooling compared to 30-40% historically. The Oregon Legislature attempted to expand access through [SB 1536](/legislation/2022-regular-session/sb-1536) in 2022, allowing renters to install cooling units, and debated [SB 54](/legislation/2025-regular-session/sb-54) in 2025 to require cooling solutions in larger buildings, though it died in committee. State and local programs, including [Portland](/cities/portland)'s Clean Energy Fund, have distributed over 20,000 heat pumps while state incentives have supported 6,000 installations, as vulnerable populations including the elderly and those with chronic conditions face increasing heat-related health risks.
