[Home Forward](/organizations/home-forward) CEO Ivory Mathews resigned following reporting on her $100,000+ travel spending while the state's largest housing authority struggled with 955 vacant units and deteriorating finances, with Mayor [Keith Wilson](/people/keith-wilson) set to approve her $171,424.80 severance package. Multnomah County Commissioner Shannon Singleton and [Portland](/cities/portland) City Councilor Eric Zimmerman are calling for the entire nine-member Home Forward board to resign or be reinterviewed, citing the board's failure to provide oversight as the agency's performance declined. The board, which defended Mathews until her resignation, is appointed through recommendations from Portland, Gresham, and Multnomah County to the mayor, with final confirmation by Portland City Council.
