Amazon will pay $20.5 million to settle with northeast Oregon residents affected by nitrate-contaminated groundwater, though the company denies contributing to pollution linked to wastewater from its data centers at the Port of Morrow. The settlement is part of a larger lawsuit filed by Morrow County residents seeking class action status against 17 defendants, including major food processors and utilities, which also demands establishment of a state-backed cleanup and health monitoring program. Governor [Tina Kotek](/people/tina-kotek) visited affected Boardman residents in 2023 promising action, nearly two years after the county declared an emergency over contamination that the state first acknowledged 30 years ago, with at least 634 wells now containing unsafe nitrate levels.
