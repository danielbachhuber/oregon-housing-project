Oregon Gov. [Tina Kotek](/people/tina-kotek) and [Portland](/cities/portland) officials have heavily invested in the Low-Income Housing Tax Credit program to address homelessness, but economists warn the federal subsidy creates apartments priced around $1,400/month that are too expensive for the poorest residents while competing with market-rate housing—leaving nearly 2,000 subsidized units vacant. Despite decades of academic criticism showing rental vouchers could help twice as many people per dollar spent, the program has bipartisan support in Congress, including from Oregon Sen. Ron Wyden who advocates expanding rather than reforming it. The mismatch has fueled Oregon's affordable housing boom to nearly 5,000 units annually while rents, home prices, and homelessness continue rising faster than incomes.
