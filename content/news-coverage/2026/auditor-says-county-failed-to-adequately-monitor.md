Multnomah County Auditor Jennifer McGuirk blamed county commissioners for failing to monitor homeless services nonprofit Sunstone Way after it overbilled the county by $525,000 in 2022, alleging the county ignored her recommendations for stricter financial oversight that could have prevented the organization's collapse. The nonprofit, which operated three pod villages and two homeless shelters for [Portland](/cities/portland) and the county, announced in March it would cease operations and lay off all 175 staff members after its former finance manager sued for retaliation over questionable spending practices. The county's Homeless Services Department disputed McGuirk's criticism, claiming it implemented the recommended safeguards but still failed to detect Sunstone Way's financial distress before the organization folded.
