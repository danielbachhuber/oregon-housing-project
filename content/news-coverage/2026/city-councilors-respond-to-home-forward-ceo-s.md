[Portland](/cities/portland) city councilors are criticizing [Home Forward](/organizations/home-forward) CEO Ivory Mathews' $100,000+ travel spending between 2023 and 2025, with Councilor Steve Novick saying it "doesn't pass the smell test" and Mayor [Keith Wilson](/people/keith-wilson) calling for responsible use of public funds. The controversy intensified after reports revealed Mathews spent an average of 45 days per year traveling to conferences—including taking 10 staff members to Orlando where some campaigned for her national board position—while the housing authority struggled with 955 vacant units and a 185-day average to fill them. Though Mathews and Home Forward's board have defended the travel as necessary for staying connected to national best practices, the union representing more than half the agency's employees has declared no confidence in her leadership.
