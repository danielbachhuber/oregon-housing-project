Douglas County will receive a $400,000 federal Community Development Block Grant through the city of Roseburg to fund health, safety, and accessibility repairs for low-income homeowners, with NeighborWorks Umpqua administering the program. The grant, managed by the Oregon Business Development Department, is expected to serve 17 to 23 households over two years, providing up to $20,000 per home for repairs like roof replacements, bathroom accessibility modifications, and other critical improvements for seniors, veterans, and people with disabilities earning 80% or less of area median income. The program aims to address housing repair needs in rural areas where residents typically have less access to such resources compared to urban communities.
