Oregon's housing supply grew only 5% between 2020 and 2025, reaching 1.9 million units, with just 14,000 new units built in 2024-2025—far short of Gov. [Tina Kotek](/people/tina-kotek)'s goal of 36,000 units annually set after declaring a homelessness emergency in 2023. Central Oregon saw the strongest growth with Deschutes and Crook counties adding 11.6% and 10.6% respectively, while [Portland](/cities/portland) lagged behind its surrounding areas. The persistent housing shortage continues to drive rent burdens affecting nearly half of Oregon renters and contributes to the state's ongoing homelessness crisis.
