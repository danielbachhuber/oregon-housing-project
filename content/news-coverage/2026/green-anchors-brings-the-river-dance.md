Green Anchors, a 7-acre former shipyard in North [Portland](/cities/portland), has transformed into a riverfront arts hub hosting music festivals and cultural events after cousins Mark Fisher and Matt Stein began leasing the property 14 years ago. The site, which now includes gardens, art studios, tiny houses, and an amphitheater, faces unique challenges as part of the Portland Harbor Superfund site, making improvements more expensive and requiring additional government oversight despite receiving a "no further action" letter from the Department of Environmental Quality. Fisher continues developing the property as an artists' space despite financial challenges, with the venue already operating and hosting events like the "Soulstice" festival in summer 2025.
