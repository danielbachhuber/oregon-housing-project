JT Flowers, senior adviser for Albina Vision Trust, is working to rebuild Northeast [Portland](/cities/portland)'s historically Black Albina neighborhood after displacement caused by mid-century construction projects and rising property values pushed out the community. The nonprofit has purchased several parcels including the Paramount Apartments and is acquiring the 10.5-acre Portland Public Schools headquarters, with plans to develop 1,000 homes within five years and house 2,500 to 3,000 people within a decade. Flowers, who grew up in the area before attending Yale and Oxford, returned to find his childhood neighborhood transformed and now leads efforts to center Black and working-class residents in redevelopment.
