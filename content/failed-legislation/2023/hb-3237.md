# HB 3237: Applicant Screening Fee Caps

## Overview

House Bill 3237 would have allowed local governments to adopt maximum applicant screening charges for residential tenants. The bill aimed to address concerns about high screening fees that create barriers for prospective renters, particularly low-income applicants.

## Why It Failed

The bill was referred to the House Committee on Housing and Homelessness but never advanced to a floor vote.

**Final Status**: Died in House Committee on Housing and Homelessness upon adjournment (June 25, 2023)

## Key Details

The bill would have:
- Authorized local governments to set maximum applicant screening charges
- Exempted third-party screening reports that are available to multiple landlords
- Given cities and counties local control over screening fee regulations

This issue has re-emerged in subsequent legislative sessions, indicating ongoing concern about screening fees as a barrier to housing access.

## Supporters

- Tenant advocacy organizations
- Housing affordability advocates

## Opponents

- Landlord associations (likely)
- Property management industry (likely)
