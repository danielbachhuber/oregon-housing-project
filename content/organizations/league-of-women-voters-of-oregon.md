# League of Women Voters of Oregon

The League of Women Voters of Oregon (LWVOR) is a nonpartisan civic organization founded in 1920, shortly after the passage of the 19th Amendment. Based in Salem, LWVOR is a state affiliate of the national League of Women Voters and operates as a 501(c)(4) nonprofit.[^1]

LWVOR's mission is to empower voters and defend democracy through advocacy, education, and civic participation. While the organization addresses a broad range of policy areas, it has been an active participant in Oregon housing and land use policy debates, regularly testifying before the Oregon Legislature on bills related to affordable housing, manufactured housing, homelessness, and land use planning.[^2]

## Policy Positions

- **Land use planning**: Supports Oregon's statewide land use planning program and the development and maintenance of local comprehensive plans and development codes, with adequate funding for effective management and enforcement.[^3]

- **Affordable housing funding**: Advocates for state investment in affordable housing production and preservation, including bond funding and appropriations for housing programs administered by [Oregon Housing and Community Services](/organizations/oregon-housing-and-community-services).[^4]

- **Manufactured housing protections**: Supports protections for manufactured home park residents, including limits on rent increases for homeowners in manufactured dwelling parks and marinas.[^5]

- **Homelessness prevention**: Supports funding and programs to address homelessness, including services for unaccompanied homeless youth and emergency housing assistance.[^4]

- **Publicly supported housing transparency**: Supports requirements for landlords of publicly supported housing to provide tenants with adequate notice when affordability restrictions are set to expire.[^6]

- **Citizen involvement in land use**: Supports meaningful public participation in land use decision-making processes, including adequate public notice and opportunities for review and comment at each phase of policy development.[^3]

## Key Legislative Involvement

- **[SB 973](/legislation/2025/sb-973) (2025)**: Testified in support of requiring landlords of publicly supported housing to give tenants 30 months' notice before affordability restrictions expire, extending the previous 20-month requirement.[^6]

- **[HB 3054](/legislation/2025/hb-3054) (2025)**: Testified in support of limiting rent increases for manufactured home park and marina residents to six percent, citing the importance of protecting this source of naturally occurring affordable housing.[^5]

- **[SB 1530](/legislation/2024/sb-1530) (2024)**: Testified in support of the omnibus housing appropriations bill, which funded programs administered by Oregon Housing and Community Services and other state agencies.[^7]

- **HB 4064 (2022)**: Testified in support of expanding eligibility for siting manufactured homes and prefabricated structures in residential zones, removing local government barriers to manufactured housing.[^8]

- **[SB 391](/legislation/2021/sb-391) (2021)**: Testified in support of authorizing counties to allow accessory dwelling units in rural residential zones, expanding housing options outside urban growth boundaries.[^9]

- **[HB 2578](/legislation/2021/hb-2578) (2021)**: Testified in support of phasing out the mortgage interest deduction for non-primary residences and redirecting revenue to an Oregon Housing Opportunity Account.[^10]
