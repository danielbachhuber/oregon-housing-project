# DevNW

DevNW is a nonprofit community development organization based in Eugene, Oregon, focused on affordable housing, financial education, and homeownership services. The organization was formed in 2019 through the merger of Neighborhood Economic Development Corporation (NEDCO) and Willamette Neighborhood Housing Services (WNHS), combining over 60 years of community development experience.[^1]

DevNW builds and manages over 400 units of affordable rental housing across Lane, Benton, and Linn counties, and has built and sold more than 155 homes for low- and moderate-income first-time homebuyers.[^2] The organization also operates Community LendingWorks, an affiliated Community Development Financial Institution (CDFI) that provides loans to underserved individuals and small businesses.[^3] DevNW is a member of the NeighborWorks network and a HUD-approved homeownership counseling agency.[^4]

## Policy Positions

- **Affordable homeownership investment**: Advocates for increased state funding for programs that help Oregonians build stability and equity through homeownership, especially first-time homebuyers and communities historically excluded from ownership.[^5]
- **Housing funding**: Supports robust state budgets for [Oregon Housing and Community Services](/organizations/oregon-housing-and-community-services) and increased investment in affordable housing development and preservation.[^6]
- **Homeownership development programs**: Supports expanding and improving OHCS homeownership development incubator programs to increase affordable homeownership pipelines.[^7]
- **Middle housing and land division**: Supports policies enabling land division for middle housing to expand homeownership opportunities in Oregon cities.[^8]
- **Foreclosure and consumer protections**: Advocates for protections for homeowners facing foreclosure, including COVID-19 pandemic-era mortgage relief and surplus recovery from tax-foreclosed properties.[^9]
- **Housing lending tax credits**: Supports expanding tax credits for affordable housing lending, including credits for loans to homebuyers and for preservation of distressed properties.[^10]

## Key Legislative Involvement

- **[HB 2258](/legislation/2025-regular-session/hb-2258) (2025)**: Testified in support of preapproved residential building plans to streamline housing production.[^11]
- **[HB 2698](/legislation/2025-regular-session/hb-2698) (2025)**: Testified in support of establishing a statewide homeownership goal, with multiple rounds of testimony advocating for the policy.[^12]
- **[HB 3236](/legislation/2025-regular-session/hb-3236) (2025)**: Testified in support of expanding tax credits for affordable housing lending on multiple occasions throughout the session.[^10]
- **HB 3090 (2025)**: Testified in support of directing OHCS to amend its homeownership development incubator program.[^7]
- **[HB 3414](/legislation/2023-regular-session/hb-3414) (2023)**: Testified in support of Governor Kotek's housing production bill to streamline local implementation of housing laws and establish the Housing Accountability and Production Office.[^13]
- **[SB 458](/legislation/2021-regular-session/sb-458) (2021)**: Testified in support of allowing land division to separate dwelling units for middle housing in Oregon cities.[^8]
- **[HB 2009](/legislation/2021-regular-session/hb-2009) (2021)**: Testified multiple times in support of COVID-19 pandemic foreclosure protections for Oregon homeowners and renters.[^9]
