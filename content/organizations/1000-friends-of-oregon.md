# 1000 Friends of Oregon

1000 Friends of Oregon is a nonprofit land use and housing advocacy organization founded in 1975 by Governor Tom McCall and attorney Henry Richmond.[^1] Based in [Portland](/cities/portland), the organization was created to champion and defend Oregon's statewide land use planning system established by [SB 100](/legislation/1973/sb-100) in 1973.

Over its 50-year history, 1000 Friends has expanded from land use enforcement into housing policy, becoming a leading advocate for middle housing, infill development, and zoning reform. The organization works with community members, planners, and partner organizations statewide to close Oregon's housing gap without creating sprawl development.[^2] Mary Kyle McCurdy serves as Associate Director and Managing Attorney and is the organization's primary legislative lead and frequent testifier.[^3]

## Policy Positions

- **Middle housing and zoning reform**: Supports expanding housing options by re-legalizing duplexes, triplexes, fourplexes, townhomes, and cottage clusters in single-family zones.[^2]
- **Infill development**: Advocates for building housing within existing urban growth boundaries before expanding onto farm and forest land.[^4]
- **Urban growth boundary protection**: Supports maintaining strong UGBs as a cornerstone of Oregon's land use system, while allowing carefully justified expansions for affordable housing.[^5]
- **Antidisplacement**: Works to ensure housing development does not displace existing residents, particularly in underserved communities.[^2]
- **Mixed-income housing**: Supports public-private partnerships and financing tools to develop housing that integrates affordable, workforce, and market-rate units.[^6]
- **Farmland and forest protection**: Opposes development proposals that would bypass SB 100 protections or convert resource land without adequate justification.[^7]

## Key Legislative Involvement

- **[HB 4035](/legislation/2026-regular-session/hb-4035) and [HB 4082](/legislation/2026-regular-session/hb-4082) (2026)**: Testified on bills expanding urban growth boundary expansion criteria for affordable housing, expressing concerns about prioritizing development within existing boundaries.[^5]
- **SB 1564 (2026)**: Opposed the original proposal that would have bypassed standard UGB expansion procedures for [Woodburn](/cities/woodburn).[^5]
- **2025 session**: Testified on numerous housing and land use bills including [HB 2138](/legislation/2025-regular-session/hb-2138), [HB 2400](/legislation/2025-regular-session/hb-2400), HB 3145, [SB 51](/legislation/2025-regular-session/sb-51), [SB 77](/legislation/2025-regular-session/sb-77), and [SB 444](/legislation/2025-regular-session/sb-444), focusing on middle housing production and infrastructure for residential infill development.[^8]
- **HB 2001 (2023)**: Played a central role in the passage of middle housing requirements, assembling and leading a coalition of more than 25 partner organizations including [AARP Oregon](/organizations/aarp-oregon), Oregon Home Builders Association, [Habitat for Humanity of Oregon](/organizations/habitat-for-humanity-of-oregon), and [Oregon Association of Realtors](/organizations/oregon-association-of-realtors).[^9]
- **HB 2001 (2019)**: Instrumental in passing Oregon's landmark middle housing bill, which eliminated exclusionary single-family zoning in cities statewide. Described the bill as the most important housing legislation in Oregon since SB 100.[^10]
- **Housing affordability litigation (2022)**: Won a precedent-setting legal battle with Thrive Hood River over housing affordability and local government compliance with housing planning requirements.[^11]
