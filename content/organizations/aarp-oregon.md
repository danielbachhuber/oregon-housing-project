# AARP Oregon

AARP Oregon is the state office of AARP, the national nonprofit organization representing the interests of Americans aged 50 and older. With approximately 500,000 members in Oregon, AARP Oregon advocates at the state legislature on issues including health care, financial security, and housing.[^1]

In housing policy, AARP Oregon has been a consistent advocate for expanding housing options that allow older Oregonians to age in place. The organization supports middle housing, accessory dwelling units, housing accessibility standards, and tenant protections, framing these issues through the lens of livable communities for residents of all ages.[^2]

## Policy Positions

- **Middle housing and zoning reform**: Supports expanding middle housing options, including duplexes, triplexes, and cottage clusters, in residential zones to increase housing choices for older adults seeking to downsize or age in community.[^2]
- **Accessory dwelling units**: Advocates for reducing barriers to ADU construction as an affordable, accessible housing option that allows multigenerational living and aging in place.[^3]
- **Housing accessibility**: Champions accessibility standards for state-funded housing, including no-step entries, single-floor living, and wider doorways, and supports requiring [Oregon Housing and Community Services](/organizations/oregon-housing-and-community-services) to track accessible units.[^4]
- **Tenant protections**: Supports rent stabilization, eviction prevention programs, and protections for renters using non-electronic payment methods.[^5]
- **Affordable housing production**: Supports increased funding for affordable housing construction, including manufactured housing and housing for older persons within expanded urban growth boundaries.[^6]
- **Property tax foreclosure protections**: Supports ensuring former homeowners can claim surplus proceeds from tax foreclosure sales, protecting older adults from losing home equity.[^7]

## Key Legislative Involvement

- **[HB 4082](/legislation/2026/hb-4082) (2026)**: Testified in support of expanding Oregon's temporary urban growth boundary addition program to include sites for manufactured dwellings and housing predominantly for older persons affordable at 120% of area median income.[^6]
- **[SB 1576](/legislation/2026/sb-1576) (2026)**: Testified in support of requiring accessibility standards for state-funded rental housing, aligning the state building code with Fair Housing Act accessibility requirements.[^4]
- **[SB 1523](/legislation/2026/sb-1523) (2026)**: Testified in support of requiring landlords to provide non-electronic alternatives to tenant portals for submitting applications and making rent payments.[^5]
- **[HB 2138](/legislation/2025/hb-2138) (2025)**: Testified in support of expanding middle housing allowances and extending middle housing requirements to urban unincorporated lands.[^8]
- **[SB 973](/legislation/2025/sb-973) (2025)**: Testified in support of extending the notice period landlords must give tenants of publicly supported housing when affordability restrictions are expiring.[^9]
- **SB 1537 (2024)**: Testified in support of creating the Housing Accountability and Production Office and expanding housing production tools, including streamlined urban growth boundary amendments.[^10]
- **[HB 3309](/legislation/2023/hb-3309) (2023)**: Testified in support of directing Oregon Housing and Community Services to report on accessible dwelling units and increase the quantity of accessible housing.[^4]
- **HB 2001 (2019)**: Part of the coalition of more than 25 organizations, led by [1000 Friends of Oregon](/organizations/1000-friends-of-oregon), that supported Oregon's landmark middle housing bill eliminating exclusionary single-family zoning statewide.[^11]
